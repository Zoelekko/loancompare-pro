Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about personal loans and our comparison service

What is the average personal loan interest rate in 2026?
The average personal loan interest rate in 2026 ranges from 6.5% to 36% APR, depending on your credit score, loan amount, and lender. Borrowers with excellent credit (750+) can qualify for rates as low as 5.99% APR, while those with fair credit (580-669) typically see rates between 17% and 30% APR. Online lenders tend to offer more competitive rates than traditional banks.
How do I compare personal loans effectively?
To compare personal loans effectively, look beyond just the interest rate. Consider the APR (which includes fees), loan term length, origination fees (typically 1-8% of the loan amount), prepayment penalties, and monthly payment amount. Use our free loan calculator above to compare the total cost of different loan options side by side.
What credit score do I need for a personal loan?
Most lenders require a minimum credit score of 580-660 for personal loans. However, borrowers with scores above 720 receive the best rates and terms. Some online lenders like Upstart accept scores as low as 300 by using alternative data such as education and employment history. The higher your credit score, the lower your interest rate will be.
Does applying for a personal loan hurt my credit score?
Most lenders offer a pre-qualification process that uses a soft credit check, which does NOT affect your credit score. However, once you formally apply and the lender performs a hard credit inquiry, it may temporarily lower your score by 5-10 points. This impact typically fades within a few months. Multiple hard inquiries for the same type of loan within a 14-45 day window are usually counted as a single inquiry.
How long does it take to get a personal loan?
Online lenders like SoFi and LightStream can approve and fund personal loans within 1-3 business days. Some even offer same-day funding. Traditional banks and credit unions may take 1-2 weeks. The speed depends on the lender, your application completeness, and whether additional documentation is needed for verification.
